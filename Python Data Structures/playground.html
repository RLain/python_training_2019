The Financial Intelligence Centre Act (FICA) was implemented in 2001 in order to combat money laundering and the financing of terrorism. In order to comply with this act, we'll need to verify your identity. 

The way we verify FICA varies depending on which method you receive your card reader. To check which method applies to you, head to the relevant section below:

IMPORTANT: Please note FICA is for your personal capacity not your business.

What if I don't send my FICA documents?

If we do not receive your FICA documents we will not be able to settle your account (you will not receive the funds you have transacted). This is done for the protection of business owners by ensuring that people are not transacting under false identities. 

Which documents do we accept?

  1. ID document

    This must be a clear certified copy of your Barcoded South African ID. We will also accept the your Passport if your ID is lost, stolen or not yet issued.

    If you are signing up as a company and have a foreign Passport please provide this for FICA.

  2. Proof of residential address documents

    Any of the following documents will work as long as they have your full name and complete residential address on them. If the original document has a letter head please ensure this is legible when scanning or photographing. Please note this document is for your personal capacity, not your business and we do not accept documents for PO Box addresses.

    Documents less than 3 months old:

    • Bank statement 
    • Body corporate or share block correspondence 
    • Cellphone account
    • Document issued in the name of a trust 
    • Rates and taxes 
    • Retail credit account 
    • Telkom account or other fixed line service provider

    Documents less than 6 months old:

    • Home loan statement from your bank

    Documents less than 1 year old:

    • Insurance policy
    • Motor vehicle licence
    • Tax return and any official document issued by SARS
    • TV license statement
    • Home Lease/ rental agreement. This needs to show:
      • Your name
      • Your Landlord's name
      • Physical address
      • The validity period
      • The signature page signed by both parties

I don’t have any of the above!

If you don't have one of the documents listed above you can ask a family member/ landlord to complete a Declaration Form. This form can be used if you are unable to provide any other type of proof of residential address listed here, and you live with someone who can provide this proof.

  • The declarer must complete the FICA Declaration form. 
  • The declarer must include a copy of their ID and an original proof of residential address document listed above.

If your Wireless Card Reader isn't reading chip cards, here are some troubleshooting tips that might help fix the problem:

  1. First, please make sure you're entering the card into the reader the correct way around. The card should be facing up, numbers visible with the chip inside the card reader as illustrated below.

  2. If you're definitely entering the card correctly and the reader is still not reading chip cards, please retry the transaction with a different card. The chips on cards wear down with constant use and can also get damaged.
  3. If a new card is also not working, please take a torch or bright light, and look inside of the reader slot, to see if there is anything stuck inside.

    IMPORTANT: Card readers carried loose in bags can get paper, tissue or tiny objects stuck inside of them. If there is something inside, please try and remove it verygently.

  4. If you've tried a new card, and nothing is stuck inside the reader slot, and it's still not working, please get in touch with us. If your reader is still under its 1-year warranty (from the date you received it), we'll organise a replacement.

Your Wireless Card Reader power button is what you use to power up and power down your card reader. On the Wireless Pro, it's the blue button on the top right of the reader, and on the Wireless Lite, it's the black button to the left of the card reader.

Sometimes, for a variety of reasons, this power button can get stuck.

What happens when the button is stuck?

When the power button gets stuck inside the reader the reader gets caught in a limbo state. 

You won't be able to transact with the reader.

You won't be able to reset the reader.

The green lights on the top of the reader will flash whilst the reader has battery power.

Once the battery has drained you won't be able to turn it on.

Causes, and how to fix it

We will first outline how power buttons get stuck inside the reader. The power on the Wireless Pro sits flush with the casing of the reader. This means when the button is depressed and moved at an angle it can get stuck under the lip of the power button hole.

This happens when a smaller object that the button is used to turn the reader on/off such as:

 A finger Nail

 A pen

When the button gets stuck, we don't recommend using a pen, knife or similarly sized object to try and force it out. This will permanently damage the power button and create rough edges, which will get stuck time and time again.

As illustrated below, to get the button unstuck, simply use your nail and push the button down, and then back.

Once you've unstuck the button, make sure you're only using the pad of your finger to turn the reader on and off. This will prevent the button from moving under the casing lip.

It's very rare for power buttons on the Wireless Lite to get stuck as the structure of the button is different. In the event the button does get stuck, please try the same method.

IMPORTANT: If you're not able to get the button unstuck yourself, please get in touch with us. Our logistics team will arrange a refurbished replacement card reader for you, and collect the reader with the stuck button for assessment.
